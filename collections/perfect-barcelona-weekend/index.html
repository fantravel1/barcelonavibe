Perfect Weekend

Two days, one unforgettable city. This is how to do Barcelona right when time is short.

48-72 Hours Must-Sees Local Tips Balanced
Day 1

Saturday

Morning (9am-1pm)

  • 9:00 — Coffee and croissant at a local café (skip hotel breakfast)
  • 10:00 — Sagrada Família (book 10am slot online, arrive 9:45)
  • 12:00 — Walk down Passeig de Gràcia, see Casa Batlló and La Pedrera facades
  • 12:30 — Vermut hour at a terrace in Gràcia

Afternoon (1pm-7pm)

  • 14:00 — Late lunch in Gràcia (Cal Pep or any local spot)
  • 16:00 — Wander Gràcia's plazas: Plaça del Sol, Plaça de la Virreina
  • 17:00 — Park Güell (book monumental zone, or enjoy free areas)
  • 19:00 — Golden hour at Bunkers del Carmel for sunset

Night (8pm-late)

  • 21:00 — Dinner in El Born (reservations recommended)
  • 23:00 — Cocktails in El Born or Raval
  • 01:00 — Club if you're up for it (Razzmatazz, Apolo, Nitsa)
Day 2

Sunday

Morning (10am-2pm)

  • 10:00 — Sleep in (you earned it), then brunch in Barceloneta
  • 12:00 — Beach walk along Barceloneta to Port Olímpic
  • 13:00 — Vermut at a chiringuito (beach bar)

Afternoon (2pm-7pm)

  • 14:30 — Seafood lunch (paella or fideuà) in Barceloneta
  • 16:30 — Gothic Quarter exploration: Cathedral, Plaça Reial, hidden squares
  • 18:00 — El Born: Santa Maria del Mar, Picasso Museum area
  • 19:00 — Rooftop drinks with views (Hotel Ohla, Grand Hotel Central)

Evening (8pm-late)

  • 21:00 — Final dinner: splurge on something special or keep it casual with tapas
  • 23:00 — Night walk through illuminated Gothic Quarter
  • 00:00 — Last drink at a hidden bar in El Raval

Pro Tips

Book Ahead

  • Sagrada Família (essential)
  • Park Güell monumental zone
  • Saturday dinner reservation
  • Club guestlist for free entry

Skip

  • La Rambla (except to cross it)
  • Paella on La Rambla (tourist trap)
  • Overpacked schedules
  • Taxis (metro is faster)
