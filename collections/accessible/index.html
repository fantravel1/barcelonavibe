Accessible Barcelona

Barcelona has made significant accessibility improvements. Here's what you need to know.

WheelchairMobilityTransport

Transport

  • Metro: 80%+ stations have elevators (check TMB app)
  • Buses: All buses are accessible with ramps
  • Taxis: Eurotaxi adapted vehicles available
  • Airport: Fully accessible with assistance

Attractions

  • Sagrada Família: Fully accessible (not towers)
  • MNAC: Excellent accessibility
  • Picasso Museum: Accessible, lift available
  • Aquarium: Fully wheelchair accessible

Beach Access

Barcelona beaches have good accessibility infrastructure during summer season (May-September):

  • Wooden walkways to the water
  • Adapted changing facilities
  • Amphibious wheelchairs available (free, reserve ahead)
  • Accessible showers
  • Best beaches: Nova Icària, Barceloneta (near hospital)

Challenges & Tips

  • Gothic Quarter: Cobblestones and narrow streets—challenging
  • Gràcia: Hilly, uneven pavements in places
  • Eixample: Flat grid, wide sidewalks—most accessible area
  • Barceloneta: Flat, good beach access
  • Book ahead: Many attractions offer priority access
  • IMD card: EU disability card recognized for discounts
