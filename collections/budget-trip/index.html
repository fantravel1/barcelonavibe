Cheap Eats

€5-15 meals that don't feel like budget food.

Menu del Día (€10-15)

The budget traveler's secret weapon. Most restaurants offer a lunch special: starter, main, dessert, drink, bread - often under €15. Available roughly 1-4pm Monday-Friday.

  • Where to find: Any neighborhood off the tourist track
  • Best areas: Poble Sec, Sants, Gràcia, Sant Antoni
  • Avoid: Las Ramblas, Barceloneta beachfront (tourist prices)
  • Pro tip: Look for places full of workers in business casual

Markets & Bakeries

  • La Boqueria: Counter seats at back stalls (avoid tourist-facing ones)
  • Santa Caterina: Less touristy market, better prices
  • Bakeries: €2-4 for croissants, empanadas, or bocadillos
  • Supermarkets: Mercadona, Bon Preu for picnic supplies

Best Cheap Spots

  • Can Paixano: €1.50 cava, excellent seafood tapas (Barceloneta)
  • Bar Tomás: Best patatas bravas in the city, €4 (Sarrià)
  • La Cova Fumada: Tiny local joint, bombas originated here (Barceloneta)
  • Cervecería Catalana: Quality tapas, reasonable prices (Eixample)
  • Bo de B: Massive bocadillos under €6 (El Born)

Money-Saving Tips

Transport

  • T-Casual card: 10 trips for €11.35
  • Walk - Barcelona is very walkable
  • Skip taxis, use metro/bus
  • Stay central to reduce transport
  • Rent city bikes (Bicing for residents)

Attractions

  • Skip Sagrada inside (see exterior free)
  • Park Güell free zones are beautiful
  • Time museum visits for free days
  • Free walking tours (tip-based)
  • Beaches are 100% free

Drinking

  • €1.50 cava at Can Paixano
  • Supermarket wine: €3-5 bottles
  • Beach sunsets = free bar
  • Happy hours at rooftop bars
  • Pre-game before clubs

When to Visit

  • Cheapest: January-February
  • Shoulder: November, March, April
  • Avoid: August, Primavera/Sonar weeks
  • Midweek flights are cheaper
  • Book hostels 2-3 weeks ahead
