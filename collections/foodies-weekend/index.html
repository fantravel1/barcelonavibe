Foodie Weekend

Eat your way through Barcelona. Markets, tapas, fine dining—a 3-day feast for serious eaters.

TapasMarketsFine Dining

Day One

Morning

Start at La Boqueria (get there by 9am). Grab a fresh juice, explore the stalls, have a sit-down breakfast at one of the market bars.

Order: jamón ibérico, pan con tomate, café con leche.

Lunch

Walk to Sant Antoni for menú del día at a local spot. Three courses plus wine for €12-15.

Afternoon: vermouth at Bar Calders, siesta, repeat.

Dinner

Tapas crawl in Poble Sec: Start on Carrer Blai for pintxos (€1-2 each), then hit Quimet i Quimet for conservas, end at Bodega 1900 for vermouth snacks.

Day Two

Morning

Coffee at Satan's Coffee Corner (specialty coffee). Then explore Born's food shops: olive oil, chocolate, cheese.

Lunch

Santa Caterina Market. Eat at the market bar—paella, seafood, or the daily special. Less touristy than Boqueria.

Dinner

Fine dining night: Tickets (book 2 months ahead) or Disfrutar for the splurge. Or try Can Culleretes (oldest restaurant).

Day Three

  • Morning: Churros at Granja Viader, then cheese and wine shopping on Carrer Girona
  • Lunch: Seafood at Barceloneta—Can Paixano for cava and sandwiches, or La Mar Salada for proper sit-down
  • Afternoon: Beach walk, gelato at Gelaaati di Marco, wine at Bar Brutal
  • Final dinner: Back to Sant Antoni for one more tapas crawl—you'll have your favorites by now
