About Port Olímpic

Built for the 1992 Olympics, Port Olímpic is Barcelona's purpose-built party zone. A marina lined with restaurants, beach clubs that transform into nightclubs, and some of the biggest venues on the Mediterranean.

Let's be honest: this isn't where you come for "authentic Barcelona." It's touristy, commercial, and mainstream. But if you want big-name DJs, beach-to-club vibes, and a guaranteed party atmosphere, Port Olímpic delivers.

The main clubs - Pacha, Opium, Shoko - line the beachfront. Most start as restaurants/beach clubs during the day, then transform after midnight. The crowd is heavily international: tourists, stag parties, Erasmus students.

Best for: First-time clubbers, groups wanting guaranteed fun, beach-to-club transitions, international DJs, and anyone who doesn't need "local" vibes.

What to Know

Getting In

  • Guest lists: Sign up online for free/reduced entry
  • Dress code: No flip flops, no shorts, smart casual
  • Timing: Arrive after 1:30 AM for club vibes
  • Entry: €15-25 including one drink
  • ID required: Passport or ID card

Pro Tips

  • Pre-drink elsewhere - drinks are expensive
  • Thursdays are Erasmus party night
  • Taxis line up outside at closing
  • Skip the marina restaurants (tourist traps)
  • The beach is free and beautiful at sunrise
