About Sant Antoni

Sant Antoni has transformed from a quiet residential area into Barcelona's coolest neighborhood almost overnight. The renovation of the stunning Mercat de Sant Antoni in 2018 kicked off a wave of brunch spots, natural wine bars, and specialty coffee shops.

Every Sunday morning, the area around the market becomes a book and collectibles market - one of Barcelona's best local traditions. The rest of the week, it's a food lover's paradise with some of the city's best new restaurants.

Unlike other trendy neighborhoods, Sant Antoni still feels local. Families walk to the market, old bodegas serve vermouth next to craft cocktail spots, and the vibe is more neighborhood than scene.

Best for: Brunch enthusiasts, food lovers, market wanderers, coffee snobs, natural wine fans, and anyone looking for Barcelona's current moment.

Sunday Market

Every Sunday, the streets around Mercat de Sant Antoni transform into one of Barcelona's best markets. Old books, vintage comics, postcards, coins, and collectibles. It's been running since the 1800s.

When: Sundays, 8am-2pm
Best time: Arrive by 10am for browsing, then stay for vermut
