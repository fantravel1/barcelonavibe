About Sants

Most people only see Sants from inside the train station. That's a mistake. This working-class neighborhood has been here since Barcelona was industrial, and it's kept its identity despite gentrification everywhere else.

Carrer de Sants is one of Barcelona's longest pedestrian shopping streets. Local shops, no chains. The area around Plaça de Sants has excellent local bars where a caña costs €2. In August, the Festa Major de Sants is one of Barcelona's best neighborhood festivals - streets compete to build the most elaborate decorations.

Best for: Budget travelers staying near the station, authentic local atmosphere, Festa Major in August, and escaping the tourist center.

Festa Major de Sants

Every August (around the 24th), Sants throws one of Barcelona's best neighborhood festivals. Streets compete to create the most elaborate themed decorations - entire blocks transformed into underwater worlds, jungles, or fantasy lands.

Live music every night, giant communal dinners in the streets, and a atmosphere of community celebration that's rare in modern cities. It's free, it's local, and it's absolutely worth planning your trip around.

When: Around August 24th (check exact dates). Where: Centered on Plaça de Sants and surrounding streets.
