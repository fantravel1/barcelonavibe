About Sarrià

Sarrià was an independent village until 1921. You can still feel it. Narrow streets, a proper village square (Plaça de Sarrià), local shops that have been there for generations, and absolutely no tourist infrastructure. This is where Barcelona's old money lives.

The neighborhood is split into distinct areas: Sarrià (the old village), Sant Gervasi (more urban, great nightlife for locals), and the hills leading to Tibidabo. CosmoCaixa science museum is up here, as are some of Barcelona's best schools and most expensive real estate.

Best for: Escaping tourist Barcelona, excellent restaurants, family activities, CosmoCaixa museum, and seeing how locals actually live.

Getting There

FGC Train

Take FGC from Plaça Catalunya to Sarrià station (15 min). This is the best option. The train runs through the hills with nice views.

Bus

Bus 66 from Plaça Catalunya goes through Sarrià. Takes longer but you see more of the neighborhood.
