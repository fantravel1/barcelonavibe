About Poblenou

Poblenou was once Barcelona's industrial heart - the "Manchester of Catalonia" with textile factories lining every street. Today, it's been reborn as the 22@ innovation district, home to tech startups, creative agencies, and some of the city's most exciting new ventures.

The transformation preserved the industrial architecture, so you'll find art galleries in former warehouses, restaurants in old factories, and one of Europe's best club complexes - Razzmatazz - in a converted industrial space.

The Rambla del Poblenou is a peaceful tree-lined street with local cafes and restaurants. The beaches here (Bogatell, Mar Bella) are quieter than Barceloneta. And there's a village feel that's increasingly rare in Barcelona.

Best for: Clubbers (Razzmatazz is unmissable), creatives, digital nomads, beach lovers who hate crowds, and anyone interested in Barcelona's innovative side.
