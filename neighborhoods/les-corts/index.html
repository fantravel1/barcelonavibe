About Les Corts

Les Corts is where Barcelona actually lives. A middle-class residential neighborhood, it's dominated by one thing: Camp Nou, the largest football stadium in Europe. On match days, the area transforms. Otherwise, it's quiet streets, local bars, and family life.

Beyond football, there's L'Illa Diagonal (major shopping center), some of Barcelona's best local restaurants, and the feel of a city that's not performing for tourists. The area is undergoing changes as Camp Nou gets renovated - expect construction until 2026.

Best for: Football fans, those seeking authentic local neighborhoods, shoppers, and anyone tired of tourist Barcelona.

Camp Nou Experience

Match Day

  • Book tickets on fcbarcelona.com (resale is risky)
  • Arrive 1-2 hours early for atmosphere
  • Bars around Travessera de Les Corts fill up
  • Metro Collblanc or Les Corts (L5) or Maria Cristina (L3)
  • Big matches sell out weeks ahead

Stadium Tour

  • Camp Nou Experience tour + museum
  • €28-35 depending on tier
  • Book online to skip queues
  • 2-3 hours for full experience
  • Note: limited access during renovation
