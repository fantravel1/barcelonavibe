About Eixample

Eixample (pronounced "ay-SHAM-pluh," meaning "expansion") is Barcelona's elegant 19th-century grid neighborhood. Built when the medieval walls came down, it's famous for its wide tree-lined avenues, chamfered corners, and the world's greatest concentration of Art Nouveau (Modernista) architecture.

Passeig de Gràcia is the main artery - Barcelona's Champs-Élysées - lined with luxury shops and three Gaudí buildings: Casa Batlló, Casa Milà (La Pedrera), and Casa Amatller. But the neighborhood is much more than architecture tourism.

Gayxample (the left side of Eixample) is Spain's LGBTQ+ capital - a welcoming, vibrant area packed with gay bars, clubs, and queer-friendly businesses. The scene is legendary, from bear bars to dance clubs to the famous rooftop scene.

Best for: LGBTQ+ travelers, architecture lovers, foodies seeking high-end dining, rooftop bar enthusiasts, shoppers, and anyone wanting Barcelona's more elegant side.

Must-See Buildings

  • Casa Batlló - Gaudí's dragon-like facade
  • La Pedrera - Gaudí's undulating masterpiece
  • Casa Amatller - Puig i Cadafalch's chocolate house
  • Hospital Sant Pau - UNESCO World Heritage site
  • Casa Lleó Morera - Complete the Block of Discord

Best Times to Visit

  • Casa Batlló - Early morning or magic nights
  • La Pedrera - Sunset rooftop tours
  • Hospital Sant Pau - Weekday mornings
  • Walking the grid - Any time, architecture everywhere
