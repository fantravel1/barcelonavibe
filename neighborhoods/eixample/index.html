Neighborhood Guide

Eixample The Modernist Grid

Barcelona's planned expansion district. Gaudí masterpieces, modernist architecture, upscale shopping, wide tree-lined avenues.

The Vibe

Eixample is Barcelona's 19th-century masterplan. Perfect grid layout, wide avenues, modernist buildings on every corner. Home to Gaudí's greatest works. Upscale, cosmopolitan, designed.

Best for: Architecture lovers, Gaudí fans, upscale shopping, dining. Sub-neighborhoods: Dreta (east, upscale), Esquerra (west, quieter, local), Sagrada Familia area (tourist hub).

Key Landmarks

Sagrada Familia: Gaudí's unfinished basilica. Entry €20-30, book online. Allow 2-3 hours. Most visited Barcelona attraction.

Casa Batlló: Gaudí's residential masterpiece on Passeig de Gràcia. Entry €25. Colorful, organic forms.

La Pedrera (Casa Milà): Unique rooftop with chimney sculptures. Entry €20-25. City views.

Passeig de Gràcia: Main avenue. Modernist buildings, luxury shops, people-watching.

Hospital de Sant Pau: Stunning modernist complex. UNESCO site. Less crowded than Gaudí buildings.

Where to Eat & Shop

Dining: Upscale restaurants on main avenues, better-value side streets. Mix of Catalan, international. Budget €15-40.

Shopping: Luxury brands on Passeig de Gràcia. Independent boutiques on side streets. Fashion, design.

Cafés: Excellent independent cafés. Strong breakfast culture.

Getting Around

Metro: Excellent coverage. Passeig de Gràcia (L2, L3, L4), Diagonal (L3, L5), Sagrada Familia (L2, L5).

Grid layout: Easiest Barcelona neighborhood to navigate. Streets numbered logically.

Walking: Distances longer than old town. Comfortable shoes essential.

Local Tips

1. Book Sagrada Familia online - long lines otherwise.

2. Look up - architectural details above street level are stunning.

3. Side streets have better-value restaurants than main avenues.

4. Visit weekday mornings for fewer crowds at attractions.

5. Passeig de Gràcia at night is beautifully illuminated.
