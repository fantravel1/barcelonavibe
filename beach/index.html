Barcelona's Beaches

From south to north, each beach has its own personality.

🏖
Most Popular

Barceloneta Beach

The iconic city beach. Most crowded but best facilities - showers, lifeguards, chiringuitos. Volleyball, people-watching, and the W Hotel backdrop.

Busy Iconic Facilities
🌊

Sant Sebastià Beach

The quieter end near the W Hotel. Better for swimming, less crowded. Small nudist section at the far end near the breakwater.

Quieter Swimming
🏝
Local Pick

Bogatell Beach

The local's beach. Much less crowded than Barceloneta. Popular with runners, joggers, and families. Still has chiringuitos.

Local Favorite Relaxed
🌊

Mar Bella Beach

Barcelona's main nudist beach and LGBTQ+ friendly spot. More alternative crowd, watersports center, and chilled vibes.

Alternative LGBTQ+
🏞

Nova Icaria Beach

Near Port Olímpic. Good balance of facilities and space. Popular with families and groups. Close to the marina restaurants.

Balanced Families
🌅

Llevant Beach

The furthest north, near Forum. Very quiet, feels like you've escaped the city. Dog-friendly section available.

Quiet Escape

Beach Tips

Do

  • Arrive early (before 11am) or late (after 5pm)
  • Bring water - chiringuito drinks are expensive
  • Use sunscreen - Mediterranean sun is strong
  • Take valuables to the water with you
  • Try Bogatell or Mar Bella for fewer crowds

Don't

  • Buy from beach vendors (illegal, often scams)
  • Leave valuables unattended
  • Expect pristine water (it's a city beach)
  • Go on Sunday if you hate crowds
  • Trust the "massage" or "beer" sellers

Beach to Club

The classic Barcelona move: beach during the day, chiringuito at sunset, dinner, then clubs at midnight.
