Seafood Reality Check

The truth: Most beachfront restaurants serve frozen, overpriced seafood to tourists who don't know better. Real paella takes 20+ minutes to cook - if it arrives in 10, it was premade.

For the good stuff, go where locals go: traditional marisquerías, market restaurants, and the spots in Barceloneta that aren't on the paseo marítimo.

Seafood Guide

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paella: Rice with saffron, seafood or rabbit
  • Fideuà: Paella but with noodles
  • Arroz negro: Squid ink rice
  • Suquet: Catalan fish stew
  • Gambas al ajillo: Garlic shrimp
  • Percebes: Goose barnacles (expensive, worth it)

Red Flags

  • Photos of food on the menu
  • Paella ready in under 20 minutes
  • "Paella for one" (real paella is shared)
  • Touts trying to seat you from the street
  • Right on the beach promenade
  • No Spanish customers inside

FAQ

What time should I eat paella?

Paella is traditionally a lunch dish (2-4pm). Many restaurants don't serve rice at dinner. Book lunch and make paella your main meal.

How much should paella cost?

€15-25 per person at quality restaurants. Under €10 = probably frozen. Over €30 = you're paying for the view, not the food.

Do I need reservations?

Yes, especially for weekend lunch. Call ahead for 7 Portes, Can Solé, and popular spots. Give your paella order when booking - some restaurants require advance notice.
