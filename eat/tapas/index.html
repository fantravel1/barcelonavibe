Tapas Culture

In Barcelona, tapas aren't a full meal - they're a way of eating. El vermut (vermouth hour) around 1pm. L'aperitiu before dinner. Hopping between bars, standing at the counter, sharing plates.

Essential dishes: patatas bravas (fried potatoes with spicy sauce), pa amb tomàquet (bread rubbed with tomato), croquetas, jamón ibérico, boquerones (anchovies), and tortilla española.

Tapas Tips

What time should I go for tapas?

Vermut hour: 12-2pm on weekends. Pre-dinner: 7-9pm. Dinner tapas: 9-11pm. Avoid the 7pm tourist rush - locals eat later.

How much should tapas cost?

€3-8 per tapa at traditional bars. €8-15 at modern spots. If you're paying €20 for patatas bravas, you're in a tourist trap.

Can I just walk in or do I need reservations?

Traditional bars: walk in, stand at the bar. Modern tapas restaurants: book ahead for dinner, especially weekends. Cal Pep and La Pepita need reservations.
