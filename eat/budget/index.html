The Menú del Día

Spain's greatest invention: the menú del día. For €10-15, you get a full three-course lunch with drink included. First course, second course, dessert or coffee, bread, and usually wine or water.

When: Monday-Friday, 1-4pm. Some spots do weekend menus for €2-3 more.
Where: Look for chalkboards outside local bars. Avoid tourist streets.

Budget Tips

Money Savers

  • Lunch is always cheaper than dinner
  • Tap water (agua del grifo) is free and safe
  • Vermouth bars often have free tapas
  • Terraces charge €0.50-1 more per drink
  • Skip La Rambla entirely for food

Tourist Traps

  • Photos on menus = run away
  • Anyone waving you in from the street
  • €15 paella for one person
  • "Tapas" that cost €12 each
  • Sangria served in pitchers to tourists

FAQ

How much should I budget for food in Barcelona?

Budget: €25-35/day (menú del día + cheap dinner). Mid-range: €40-60/day. Comfortable: €60-80/day with some nice dinners.

Is tipping required in Barcelona?

No. Service is included. Locals might round up or leave €1-2 at nice restaurants, but it's not expected. Don't feel pressured to tip like in the US.

Where should I avoid eating?

La Rambla, Barceloneta beachfront, anywhere with photos on menus, and any place with someone outside trying to wave you in. These are tourist traps with bad food at high prices.
