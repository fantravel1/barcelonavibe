Barcelona's most civilized tradition. Vermouth, olives, and good company - the Sunday ritual that defines the city.

What is Vermut?

Fer el vermut (doing the vermouth) is Barcelona's pre-lunch ritual. Around noon on Sundays, Catalans head to their local bar for vermouth on tap, olives, and chips. It's social, relaxed, and non-negotiable.

The drink itself is fortified wine infused with herbs and botanicals. Served over ice with a splash of soda, an orange slice, and an olive. Usually €2-4 a glass. The vibe is everything.

When: 12-2pm, especially Sundays. What to order: "Un vermut, si us plau" (a vermouth, please).

How to Vermut

The Ritual

  1. Arrive around 12-1pm (earlier for good seats)
  2. Order "un vermut" at the bar or terrace
  3. Get olives, chips, or boquerones
  4. Chat, relax, watch the world
  5. Leave by 2pm for actual lunch

What to Pair

  • Olives: Always. Essential.
  • Patatas chips: Simple, perfect
  • Boquerones: Marinated anchovies
  • Conservas: Tinned seafood
  • Banderillas: Pickled skewers

Vermut FAQ

Is vermut the same as vermouth?

Yes and no. Vermut is the Catalan/Spanish word for vermouth. But Spanish vermut is typically sweeter and more aromatic than Italian or French vermouths. It's always served on ice with soda and garnishes.

Can I do vermut on weekdays?

Absolutely. Any day works, but Sunday is the tradition. Weekday vermut around 1pm before lunch is totally normal. Saturdays are also popular.

What brands should I try?

Local favorites: Yzaguirre (the classic), Padró & Co (premium), Lustau (sherry-based). Many bars have their own house vermut on tap - always try that first.
