Catalan Wine

Catalonia produces some of Spain's best wines. Priorat (powerful reds), Penedès (cava and whites), Montsant (value reds), and Empordà (Mediterranean styles). All within day-trip distance from Barcelona.

In the city, natural wine bars have exploded. Traditional bodegas serve house wine for €2-3 a glass. And cava - the local sparkling wine - is what locals actually drink instead of champagne.

Wine Regions

Priorat

Spain's most prestigious wine region after Rioja. Powerful reds from old Garnacha and Cariñena vines. 2 hours from Barcelona.

Premium

Penedès

Home of cava. Also excellent whites (Xarel·lo, Macabeo). Visit Codorníu or Freixenet caves. 45 min from Barcelona.

Cava

Montsant

"Little Priorat" - similar soils, lower prices. Excellent value reds. Often half the price of Priorat quality.

Value

Empordà

Costa Brava wines. Mediterranean rosés, Garnatxa-based reds. Less famous, excellent quality. Near Dalí country.

Coastal

Wine Tips

What is cava?

Catalan sparkling wine made using the same method as Champagne. Most comes from Penedès. Ranges from €5 supermarket to €30+ premium bottles. Locals drink cava, not champagne.

How much should wine cost?

House wine: €2-4/glass. Decent by the glass: €4-8. Natural wine bars: €6-12. Bottles: €15-40 at restaurants (markup is lower than US/UK).

Can I do wine tours from Barcelona?

Yes! Penedès (cava caves) is 45 minutes by train. Priorat needs a car or tour. Many companies offer day trips with tastings. Book ahead in summer.
