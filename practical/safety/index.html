Safety Tips

Barcelona is generally safe. The main risk is pickpockets. Here's what you actually need to know.

Main Risk

Pickpockets

The #1 issue for tourists. Know the hotspots and stay alert.

Hotspot Areas

  • La Rambla: The worst. Especially crowded sections
  • Metro: L3 (Green line), any busy station
  • Plaça Catalunya: Major transit hub
  • Barceloneta beach: Don't leave bags unattended
  • Sagrada Família area: Tourist crowds
  • Gothic Quarter: Narrow streets, easy escapes

Prevention Tips

  • Use a crossbody bag worn in front
  • Keep phone in front pocket, not back
  • Don't put phones on restaurant tables
  • Use inside jacket pockets for wallet
  • Be extra alert when distracted (photos, maps)
  • Watch for people bumping into you
  • Don't wear expensive jewelry or watches

Common Tactics

  • Distraction: Someone bumps you while another steals
  • Fake petitions: Clipboard people on La Rambla
  • Spill scam: Something spilled on you, "helper" steals
  • Metro crush: Crowded train doors
  • Beach theft: While you're swimming

Neighborhood Safety

Very Safe

  • Eixample (all of it)
  • Gràcia
  • El Born
  • Sarrià-Sant Gervasi
  • Poblenou

Safe (Normal Precautions)

  • Gothic Quarter
  • Barceloneta
  • Sant Antoni
  • Poble Sec
  • Most of Raval (daytime)

Use Caution (Late Night)

  • Lower Raval after midnight
  • La Rambla late at night
  • Empty Gothic Quarter streets
  • Plaça Reial surroundings (2-5am)

Solo Female Travelers

  • Barcelona is very safe for solo women
  • Street harassment is rare
  • Use same precautions as home
  • Stick to main streets at night
  • Share location with someone

Emergency Numbers

  • 112: General emergency (police, fire, ambulance)
  • 091: National Police
  • 092: Local Police (Guardia Urbana)
  • 061: Medical emergencies
  • 088: Mossos d'Esquadra (Catalan police)

For theft reports (insurance claims), go to any police station. Bring passport. English spoken at tourist police stations.
