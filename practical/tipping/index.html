Tipping Guide

Spain is not a heavy tipping culture. Service is included. Here's what locals actually do.

Overview

The Basics

Key point: Tipping is appreciated but not expected or required. Servers earn a living wage. There's no "15-20%" culture like in the US. Over-tipping can actually make locals uncomfortable. Round up or leave small change—that's the norm.

Restaurants

  • Casual meal: Round up or leave coins
  • Nice dinner: 5-10% if service was great
  • Fine dining: 10% maximum
  • Menú del día: Leave €1-2 or nothing
  • Service included: Check bill, often says "servicio incluido"

Bars & Cafes

  • Coffee: Not expected, leave coins if you like
  • Drinks at bar: Round up (€3.50 → €4)
  • Cocktail bar: €1 per round is generous
  • Nightclub: Not expected
  • Tapas bar: Leave small change

Other Services

  • Taxis: Round up to nearest euro. €7.50 fare → give €8. That's it.
  • Hotels: €1-2 per bag for porters. Housekeeping optional but appreciated.
  • Free walking tours: €5-10 per person is standard at the end.
  • Hairdressers: Round up or 5-10% for good service.
  • Delivery: Not expected but round up if you like.
  • Spa/Massage: 5-10% if service was exceptional.
