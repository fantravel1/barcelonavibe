Primavera Sound

Europe's biggest independent music festival. 10 days, hundreds of artists, beachside stages. The music event of the year.

Late May/June Parc del Fòrum 200,000+ Fans Indie/Alternative World-Class
Tickets

Getting In

Ticket Types

  • Full Festival: ~€275-350
  • Weekend Pass: ~€180-220
  • Day Tickets: ~€95-120 (limited)
  • VIP: €400+ (fast entry, lounge)
  • Prices increase over time
  • Often sells out months ahead

How to Buy

  • Official site: primaverasound.com
  • Tickets go on sale December/January
  • Early bird sells out in hours
  • Resale market exists but be careful
  • Official resale on DICE app
Festival

What to Expect

The Venue

  • Parc del Fòrum: Beachside concrete esplanade
  • Multiple stages facing the Mediterranean
  • Open-air but with some covered areas
  • Metro accessible (L4 El Maresme-Fòrum)
  • 10-15 minute walk from station to main area

Festival Structure

  • Primavera a la Ciutat: City shows (week before main fest)
  • Main Festival: 2 weekends + weeknights
  • Hours: Usually 5pm-6am
  • Headline acts typically 10pm-1am
  • Late night sets continue until dawn

Music Style

  • Indie, alternative, electronic, experimental
  • Mix of big names and rising artists
  • Strong electronic/dance stages
  • Hip-hop, punk, pop also represented
  • Known for diverse, quality lineups

Survival Tips

Preparation

  • Comfortable shoes (lots of concrete)
  • Light jacket (sea breeze at night)
  • Sunscreen for day stages
  • Portable phone charger
  • Earplugs for front rows
  • Cash + card (bars can have queues)

On Site

  • Download official app for schedule
  • Check clashing sets in advance
  • Food is decent but pricey
  • Free water refill stations exist
  • Lockers available (limited)
  • Night metro runs during festival
