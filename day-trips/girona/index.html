Girona

Medieval magic, colorful houses, world-class food. Game of Thrones' Braavos. A photographer's and foodie's paradise.

40 Minutes Medieval Food Scene Photography GoT Locations
Transport

Getting There

High-Speed AVE (Best)

  • From: Barcelona Sants
  • Time: 38 minutes
  • Price: €12-25 each way
  • Book on Renfe.com (cheaper in advance)
  • 20 min walk from station to old town

Regional Train (Budget)

  • Line: R11 or Regional
  • Time: 1.5 hours
  • Price: ~€8-12 each way
  • Drops you closer to old town
  • More flexible, no booking needed
See

What to Do

Must See

  • Cathedral: 90 steps, widest Gothic nave
  • Jewish Quarter: Best preserved in Europe
  • Onyar River: Colorful houses (iconic photo)
  • Arab Baths: 12th century, beautiful
  • City Walls: Walk the Passeig de la Muralla

Game of Thrones

  • Cathedral steps: Sparrow scenes (S6)
  • Arab Baths: Arya's bath house
  • Jewish Quarter: Braavos streets
  • Plaça dels Jurats: Theater scenes
  • Self-guided tour takes 2-3 hours
Food

World-Class Food

Home of El Celler de Can Roca (3 Michelin stars). The food scene punches way above its weight.

Must Try

  • Rocambolesc: Roca brothers' ice cream
  • Casa Marieta: Traditional Catalan
  • Divinum: Wine and tapas
  • El Celler: Book 11 months ahead
  • Local specialty: Xuixo pastry

Markets

  • Mercat del Lleó: Food market, lunch spots
  • Rambla de la Llibertat: Saturday market
  • Great for local products
  • Cheese, cured meats, olive oil

Day Trip Itinerary

  • 9am: AVE from Barcelona Sants
  • 9:40am: Arrive, walk to old town
  • 10am: Walk the city walls
  • 11am: Cathedral and Jewish Quarter
  • 12:30pm: Arab Baths
  • 1:30pm: Lunch (Casa Marieta or Divinum)
  • 3:30pm: Ice cream at Rocambolesc
  • 4pm: Onyar bridges, photos, shopping
  • 6pm: Train back to Barcelona
