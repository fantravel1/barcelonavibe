Costa Brava

Wild coast. Dramatic cliffs, turquoise coves, medieval villages. Dalí country. Catalonia's most beautiful coastline.

1-2 Hours Beaches Dalí Scenery Car Recommended
Destinations

Where to Go

Tossa de Mar

  • Walled old town on the sea
  • Beautiful main beach + hidden coves
  • Easy bus from Barcelona (1.5 hr)
  • Most accessible without car
  • Can get crowded in summer
Medieval + Beach

Cadaqués

  • Dalí's hometown, whitewashed village
  • Most picturesque Costa Brava town
  • Cap de Creus nearby (dramatic)
  • 2.5 hours from Barcelona
  • Worth overnight if possible
Artist Village

Figueres

  • Dalí Theatre-Museum (must see)
  • Most visited museum in Spain after Prado
  • Easy train from Barcelona (1 hr AVE)
  • Combine with Cadaqués or coast
  • Book museum tickets in advance
Surrealism

Calella de Palafrugell

  • Classic Costa Brava fishing village
  • White houses, rocky coves
  • Less touristy than Tossa
  • Nearby: Llafranc, Tamariu
  • Car really helps here
Hidden Gem
Transport

Getting There

By Car (Best)

  • Most flexibility for cove-hopping
  • Rent from Barcelona (~€40-60/day)
  • AP-7 highway then coast roads
  • Parking can be tricky in summer
  • Essential for hidden beaches

Without Car

  • Tossa de Mar: Direct bus from Barcelona Nord
  • Figueres: AVE train (1 hr)
  • Cadaqués: Bus from Figueres (1 hr)
  • Organized tours available
  • Limits beach-hopping options
Beaches

Best Coves

  • Cala Pola (Tossa): Accessible, beautiful, can be busy
  • Platja d'Aiguablava: Turquoise water, pine backdrop
  • Cala Estreta: Tiny hidden gem, hike required
  • Platja de Llafranc: Town beach, good facilities
  • Cala Montgó: Wide sandy beach, families
  • Cap de Creus coves: Wild, dramatic, for adventurers

Pro tip: The best coves require short hikes. Bring water shoes for rocky entries.
