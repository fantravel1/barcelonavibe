Figueres & Dalí

Visit Salvador Dalí's surrealist masterpiece. The Dalí Theatre-Museum is the largest surrealist object in the world—and it's only 2 hours from Barcelona.

2 Hours Art Surrealism Train
The Museum

Dalí Theatre-Museum

This isn't a museum about Dalí—it IS Dalí. He designed every room, every installation, every optical illusion. It's a complete immersion into surrealism, built in a bombed-out theater in his hometown. Dalí himself is buried in the crypt beneath the stage.

  • Highlights: Mae West room, Rain Taxi, Treasure Room
  • Time needed: 2-3 hours minimum
  • Tickets: €14 (book online, time slots required)
  • Hours: 10:30am-6pm (varies seasonally)
  • Tip: Book morning slot, combine with lunch in town
Transport

Getting There

By Train (Recommended)

  • From: Barcelona Sants or Passeig de Gràcia
  • Time: ~2 hours (regional) or 55min (AVE)
  • Cost: €13-25 each way
  • Station: Figueres Vilafant (high-speed) or Figueres (regional)
  • Walk: 15-20 min from either station

By Car

  • Route: AP-7 highway north
  • Time: ~1.5 hours
  • Tolls: ~€12 each way
  • Parking: Available near museum
  • Benefit: Combine with Cadaqués or Portlligat

Plan Your Day

Suggested Itinerary

  • 8:30am: Train from Barcelona Sants
  • 10:30am: Arrive Figueres, walk to museum
  • 11:00am: Explore Dalí Theatre-Museum
  • 1:30pm: Lunch at Hotel Duran (Dalí's favorite)
  • 3:00pm: Wander Figueres old town
  • 4:30pm: Train back to Barcelona
  • 6:30pm: Arrive Barcelona
