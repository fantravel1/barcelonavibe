Sitges

Barcelona's favorite beach escape. Better beaches, charming old town, legendary nightlife. Only 40 minutes away.

40 Minutes Beach LGBT+ Friendly Nightlife Easy Train
Transport

Getting There

By Train (Best Option)

  • From: Barcelona Passeig de Gràcia or Sants
  • Line: R2 Sud (RENFE/Rodalies)
  • Time: 35-40 minutes
  • Price: ~€5 each way (T-Casual works)
  • Frequency: Every 20-30 minutes
  • Train drops you 5 min walk from beach
  • Last train back around 11pm (check schedule)
Beaches

Where to Swim

Main Beaches

  • Platja de Sant Sebastià: Main beach, near town center
  • Platja de la Fragata: Church backdrop, photogenic
  • Platja de la Ribera: Long stretch, less crowded
  • All have good facilities, lifeguards

LGBT+ Beaches

  • Platja de la Bassa Rodona: Main gay beach
  • Platja de l'Home Mort: More secluded, clothing optional
  • Sitges has been LGBT+ friendly since the 1960s
  • Pride in June is legendary
Town

Beyond the Beach

Old Town

  • Whitewashed streets, bougainvillea
  • Church of Sant Bartomeu (clifftop)
  • Cau Ferrat Museum (Rusiñol's art)
  • Boutique shopping on Carrer Major
  • Excellent restaurants and tapas bars

Nightlife

  • Carrer del Pecat: Main bar strip
  • Mix of gay and mixed venues
  • Bars open until 3am+
  • Summer weekends are wild
  • Carnival (Feb) is unmissable

Day Trip Itinerary

  • 10am: Train from Barcelona
  • 11am: Arrive, walk to beach
  • 11am-1pm: Beach time
  • 1pm: Lunch in old town
  • 2:30pm: Explore old town, church
  • 4pm: Back to beach or museum
  • 7pm: Sunset drinks on promenade
  • 8:30pm: Dinner
  • 10pm: Train back (or stay for nightlife)
