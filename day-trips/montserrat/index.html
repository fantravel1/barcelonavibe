Montserrat

Catalonia's sacred mountain. A 1,000-year-old monastery perched on jagged peaks, stunning views, and real spiritual energy.

1 Hour Mountain Monastery Hiking Must See
Transport

Getting There

Tot Montserrat Ticket (Recommended)

The all-in-one pass includes:

  • Round-trip train from Barcelona (Plaça Espanya)
  • Cable car or rack railway up the mountain
  • Museum entry
  • Audiovisual exhibition
  • Meal at cafeteria OR €12 food voucher
  • Price: ~€55-60
  • Buy at FGC Plaça Espanya station or online

DIY Option

  • FGC R5 train from Plaça Espanya (hourly)
  • Get off at Montserrat-Aeri (cable car) or Monistrol (rack railway)
  • Cable car: More dramatic views, shorter ride
  • Rack railway: Longer, scenic, less crowds
  • Total cost DIY: ~€25-30 round trip
  • First train ~8:30am, last return ~6pm

What to Do

The Monastery

  • Basilica: Free entry, stunning interior
  • La Moreneta: Black Madonna statue (queue to touch)
  • Boys' Choir: 1pm daily (except July, some holidays)
  • Museum: Picasso, Dalí, El Greco
  • Allow 2-3 hours minimum

Hiking Trails

  • Sant Joan: 45 min up, spectacular views
  • Sant Jeroni: 2.5 hr, highest peak (1,236m)
  • Santa Cova: 1 hr round trip, easy walk
  • Funiculars access higher trails
  • Bring water and good shoes

Practical Tips

Planning

  • Go early (first train) for fewer crowds
  • Allow full day (6-8 hours)
  • Check choir schedule before going
  • Weekdays less crowded than weekends
  • Spring/fall: Best weather for hiking

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Water bottle
  • Layers (cooler at altitude)
  • Sun protection
  • Cash for vending/small shops
